## 3 Minutes. One Story. No Second Chances.
### RWA Demo Day — Hong Kong Web3 Festival

---

## PHILOSOPHY

This demo tells a story. It is NOT a feature tour. Every click answers a question. Every screen proves a point. The audience should feel one thing by the end:

**"This is real. This works. I want in."**

## CONTINGENCY PLAN

If the live demo fails (internet, wallet, platform issue):

1. **Do NOT panic. Do NOT apologize more than once.**
2. Say: *"Looks like the demo gods aren't with us today — but I recorded this earlier. Same product, same flow."*
3. Play the pre-recorded screen capture (keep it under 90 seconds, no audio — you narrate live).
4. **Never let a failed demo kill your energy.** The audience remembers confidence, not connectivity.

---

## DELIVERY NOTES

- **Speed:** Slower than you think. Every sentence needs air after it.
- **Hands:** Point at the screen when showing. Hands open to the audience when speaking.
- **Eyes:** When the product is on screen, look at the screen. When you say something important, look at the judges.
- **The $200 purchase:** This is your Steve Jobs "one more thing" moment within the demo. Build to it. Pause after it. Let the audience process what just happened.
- **The phone:** Rehearse pulling it out smoothly. It should feel casual, not staged. You're showing them the future fits in a pocket.
- **Humor:** The "I'm not selling" line only works if you've earned trust by then. If the demo went well, it will land. If not, skip it and stay serious.
- **Time:** Practice until you can do this in 2:45. You need the 15-second buffer for audience reaction and minor delays.
